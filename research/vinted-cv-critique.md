# Vinted CV Critique and Rewrite Notes

## High-level criticism

The previous web CV proved range, curiosity, and seniority, but it was not yet
optimized for a one-page recruiter PDF. It read like a strategy page around a
candidate rather than the candidate's final application document.

The strongest criticism is positioning: the target role is not asking for a
classic RPA developer first. It is asking for someone who can make ambiguous
marketing operations safe for agentic automation by defining process maps,
decision trees, data contracts, validation gates, fallback loops, and technical
briefs. The CV must therefore lead with process architecture and logic design,
then use RPA/C#/LLM evidence as proof.

## Edits applied

- Removed role-match score styling, sources, and working-draft notes from the
  visible CV.
- Reframed the opening around reliable workflow logic for AI automation.
- Promoted BA/PO-adjacent requirements support and C# automation-platform work
  into the primary experience section.
- Compressed side-project AI work into one proof point so it supports the role
  without looking like a hobby section.
- Preserved quantified evidence: 100+ RPA solutions, 50+ operations procedures,
  and 99% worked-item success on urgent automation work.
- Reduced long web sections into a one-page A4 structure with conventional
  headings, clear reverse chronology, and dense but readable bullets.

## Research basis

- Vinted role: Senior Business Process Architect, AI Automation. The posting
  emphasizes agentic AI, watertight logical blueprints, data contracts,
  decision trees, fallback loops, technical briefs, n8n, reusable documentation,
  and stakeholder consultation.
- Vinted company context: the company frames its mission as making second-hand
  the first choice and describes growth across Marketplace, Go, and Pay.
- Current market signal: the World Economic Forum's Future of Jobs 2025 material
  highlights AI, big data, analytical thinking, resilience, and lifelong
  learning as important labor-market forces. That supports a CV that shows both
  automation judgment and current AI learning.
- Resume-market signal: Harvard and MIT career guidance both favor targeted,
  specific, fact-based, easy-to-scan resumes with quantified outcomes and
  conventional structure.

## Remaining weaknesses to close

- Add one concrete C# platform example with scope, function replaced, adoption,
  or estimated license/cost impact.
- Add one concrete LLM/RPA example with volume, failure-handling pattern, and
  measurable result.
- If possible, add one n8n proof or a small workflow demo because the role names
  n8n directly.

## HR trust cleanup

- Avoided wording that sounds like an internal build note or cover-letter
  strategy.
- Removed phrases that could read as employer criticism, such as emphasizing an
  unfilled product-owner role.
- Rephrased internal platform work to avoid sounding like confidential company
  strategy while still showing C# automation-platform relevance.
- Removed company-specific targeting from public HTML metadata and the visible
  CV header.
- Tightened the AI side-project proof point so it reads as shipped AI-assisted
  delivery practice rather than a hobby-tool list.
- Reduced AI trend-language density so the CV reads as practical automation
  experience rather than trend-chasing.
